## 2.3 SCIENTIFIC FOUNDATIONS

### **Why the Savage Kernel Works (Theoretical Justification)**

**1. Renormalization Group Flow**
- The Savage Kernel has a critical point at η = 1, where the density scales as ρ(r) ~ r^(-3.5).
- Perturbations around this fixed point are marginal (neither relevant nor irrelevant).
- Small deviations in η map to observables (inner slope, outer slope, core/cusp transition).
- This makes η a "control parameter" for tuning the RG flow, analogous to the temperature in a phase transition.

**2. Finite Mass Guarantee**
- For ρ(r) ~ (1 + r/σ)^η to integrate to finite mass, we need ∫₀^∞ 4πr² ρ(r) dr < ∞.
- Asymptotic: ρ ~ r^(-ηβ) as r → ∞.
- Convergence requires ηβ > 3.
- Our β(η) = 3/max(η, 0.1) + 0.5 ensures ηβ > 3 for all physical η > 0.1.

**3. Universality in K-Space**
- If ρ(r) changes smoothly with σ_c, the Fourier transform ρ̃(k) should show a characteristic cutoff at k ~ 1/σ_c.
- Deviations from the model (residuals) will have noise that drops off steeply above k_cutoff.
- This spectral fingerprint is **not model-dependent**; any density profile with a sharp transition at σ_c will show it.

**4. Coupling to Baryons**
- The ε-normalization enforces M_dark = ε × M_bar globally, which ties dark and baryonic masses.
- This is stronger than CDM's assumption (which is passive lensing) but weaker than MOND (which is rigid feedback).
- The RAR emerges because once you enforce this scaling + a kernel with internal scale σ_c, the acceleration naturally clusters.

**5. Particle Physics Consistency (Ξ)**
